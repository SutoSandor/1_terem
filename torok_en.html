Facial Reconstruction of a Girl with an Aigrette from the Era of Turkish Occupation, Gödöllő

Prepared by:
Facial Reconstruction: Ágnes Kustár, PhD, Department of Anthropology of the Hungarian Natural History Museum and András Balikó, DLA, sculptor (private individual)
Colouring and hair: Zsuzsa Herczeg, restorer (private individual)

PREPARATION PHASE OF THE SCULPTURAL FACIAL RECONSTRUCTION

Skull
     The sculptural facial reconstruction is made on the gypsum copy of the original skull. The thorns fixed on the skull indicate the thickness of the soft tissues of the face.
I. Phases of the facial reconstruction
-    The eyes are replaced with plastic eyeballs.
-    The muscle are built up from plasticine. First the neck and masticator muscles, then the mimic muscles of the face are reconstructed. The thickness of the muscles is estimated on the basis of a scientific soft tissue thickness chart.
-    The cartilaginous framework of the nose is made from harder wax, and then the tip and wings of the nose are moulded from plastiline.
-    The location of the important morphologic (formal) points is fixed with long needles to help shape the forms of the face.
-    Finally, the reconstructed muscles are covered with the skin layer.
-    The formal features of the finished half-face can be verified on the basis of the half of the skull still left uncovered.
II. Raw face reconstruction
     There is no hair and eyebrow on the "raw" face reconstruction made of plastiline so the individual features of the face can be observed well.
III. The finished facial reconstruction
     The plaster cast made of the completed face reconstruction was painted and coloured. The hair was made from a wig based on photos of last-century hairstyle. The eyebrows and eyelashes were made with a unique design. The eyes were made alive by painting and the glittering of resin.
