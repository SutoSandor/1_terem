The letters of Krisztina Bossányi (before 1671- 1737)


After the liberation of Budapest (1686), Gödöllő had several landlords. The baroness Krisztina Bossányi became the part-owner of the settlement through her brother, László Bossányi. Their father married for the second time and took the previous owner's childless widow, Judit Mocsáry, as a wife.

Christina's first husband was György Szemere. Five children were born from their marriage. They administered their estates in Pest and Heves County (Noszvaj) together. In 1700, and then two years later they protested together at the Pest-Pilis-Solt county general meeting against the new part-owners of Gödöllő, Baron Ferenc Lew and József Verlain.

In 1709 she appeared in the sources as a widow. Her second husband was György Ottlyk. Ottlyk was a Kuruc officer in the army of Imre Thököly and then as an imperial colonel took part in the liberation of Buda. He joined the War of Independence led by Ferenc Rákóczi II., but after its fall he returned to his previous loyalty to the monarch. Krisztina Bossányi, after the death of her second husband around 1723, became a widow again. She administered her lands alone. She held to the ownership of her property in Gödöllő until her death in 1737, thus Antal Grassalkovich I. could only obtain it from her heirs.
