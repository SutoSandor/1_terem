The history of the Catholic Church in Gödöllő


The first written records related to the church history of Gödöllő is the village inventory from 1492, according to which the village had a stone church with a tower. During the Fifteen Years' War (1593-1606) Gödöllő was depopulated. In 1649, the returning residents were already of the Reformed faith. Antal Grassalkovich I., as a landlord, besides the Reformed population settled Catholic serfs, mostly craftsmen.

For his family and the Catholic population the landlord created the chapel in one of the wings of the newly built castle in the middle of the 18th century. The so-called castle chapel was consecrated in honor of St. John of Nepomuk in 1749. The first known chaplain (Pater Maurus Menyhardt) was nearly twenty years in service from 1755, and operated a school and a one-room hospital also. The chapel became an independent parsonage only at the beginning of the next century.

The construction of the church in Besnyő meant a large change in the religious life of the village. Numerous legends are related to the formation of the place of pilgrimage, but as the landlord reveals it in his will, he had vowed to raise the chapel in hope of the healing of his third wife, Terézia Klobusutzky. The construction of the chapel, planned after the model of the Loreto Chapel, began in 1759, and a wonderful event was connected to it. The cult object of the future shrine, a carved bone statue of Mary was found in the ground. János Fidler assistant bricklayer found it there. After the completion of the chapel , the patron, according to his original intention, had the exact replica - that was also touched to the original- of the Mary statue of Loreto delivered to Gödöllő. On 15 August, 1761 (Ascension day) the Bishop of Vác, Károly Salbeck consecrated the chapel, on the previous day they presented the Loreto statue. The miraculous appearance of the bone carving figurine of Our Lady and the exemplary religious life of the Countess soon attracted a considerable crowd to the shrine. The chapel became very tight, so Grassalkovich decided to build a church in front of it, and in order to take care of the shrine he had a nunnery built there in 1763 where he installed Capuchin fathers. Close to the church and the monastery he had seven serf house built and he deployed superannuated servants there. Their job was to help the increased number of pilgrims, and to take care of the environs of the church. The shrine is still a significant place of pilgrimage, since 2008 it has been serving the faithful as a basilica minor.
