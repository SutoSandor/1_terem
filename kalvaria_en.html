The Calvary


Antal Grassalkovich I. started to build the Calvary in 1771, but because of his death it was his son Antal II. who finished it in 1775. Presumably a builder from Pest, János Mayerhoffer made it in Baroque style.

It is a classic example of the lowland calvaries, where because of the absence of a hill, the Golgotha hill is substituted by a brick building. The top can be reached by stairs surrounded by pierced rococo stone railings. The sculpture group is placed there. In the middle there is Jesus on the cross, at his foot the figure of the penitent Mary Magdalene. Next to the cross of Jesus, there is Mary on left, and John on the right. On both sides of Jesus there are the crosses of the thieves, one of them is talking to Christ. The sculptures are made of stone. The statues of Mary and John are original, the others were made as part of the restoration after a storm damage in 1827.

The Calvary does not play a role in the church, there is no Stations of the Cross leading there either. Previously there was a chapel inside. Later a 1200 hectoliter red marble water reservoir was placed there. This ensured the castle's water supply. The water of the Pazsaki springs was led through clay pipes to the pool, until the springs dried out. After that the building stood empty for a long time, then at the end of the 19th century water was sent to the reservoir with the help of a steam engine, and once again they supplied the castle garden with water from here.

The Calvary is in the Erzsébet Park, it was last restored in 2011.
